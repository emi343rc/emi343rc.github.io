Enhancement 1: Software design and engineering

Narrative

Working on this enhancement has been a rewarding experience. Revisiting my old project with the knowledge I’ve gained from more advanced courses gave me a completely new perspective. Although I’ve forgotten some of the finer details of C# syntax, I now have a much stronger understanding of object-oriented programming — including concepts like classes, objects, and structured design. It’s fascinating to see how much my approach to coding has evolved since I first created this artifact.

Initially, my plan was to enhance all three games included in the original application. However, as I began implementing improvements, I realized that it wasn’t feasible to refine every game within the time available. Because of this, I decided to focus my efforts on improving the Flappy Bird game, ensuring that it was as polished and functional as possible.

This enhancement process wasn’t without challenges. The first major hurdle I faced was configuring my GitHub repository so that I could work seamlessly between my home PC and my school laptop. I ran into several errors and synchronization issues at first, but eventually managed to find an effective workflow.

The second challenge came when I restructured the Flappy Bird game into multiple classes. Once I changed the timer interval from 100ms to 16ms to achieve smoother performance, I had to rewrite large portions of the logic. Many integer values became floating-point calculations, and I needed to rework the physics behind the gameplay. Despite the obstacles, this process taught me a great deal about modular programming and the impact of design choices on system performance.
